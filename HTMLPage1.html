Laryngectomy Emergency Airway Management

Created by Richard S. Lomax, Kirtan Patel, and Talen Benes

Step 1: Call for Airway Expert Help

Look, Listen, and Feel at the Stoma

Is the patient breathing?

YES:

Apply high flow oxygen to laryngectomy stoma via infant size 2 ambu mask

NO:

Call Resuscitation Team

Begin CPR if no pulse or signs of life

Step 2: Assess laryngectomy stoma patency

(Most laryngectomy stomas will NOT have a tube in stoma)

Remove humidification cover if present or Inner Cannula if trach is present.

Can you pass a suction catheter?

YES:

The laryngectomy stoma is patent

Perform tracheal suction

Consider partial obstruction

Ventilate via stoma if not breathing

NO:

Deflate the cuff if present.

Look, listen, and feel at the laryngectomy stoma or tube.

Use waveform capnography or Mapleson C if available.

If the patient is stable or improving, continue with ABCDE assessment and apply high flow oxygen if needed.

If NOT, Proceed to Step 3.

Step 3: Remove Tube from Laryngectomy Stoma

(Look, Listen, and Feel at the laryngectomy stoma)

Look, Listen & Feel at the Laryngectomy Stoma. Ensure Oxygen is re-applied to Stoma. Use Waveform Capnography.

Proceed with CPR if No Pulse is Found

Step 4: CPR

Primary Emergency Oxygenation

Laryngectomy stoma ventilation via either Paediatric mask applied to stoma LMA applied to stoma

Secondary Emergency Oxygenation

Attempt intubation of laryngectomy stoma

Size 4 DCT trach/6.0 cuffed ETT

Consider Fiberoptic scope/Bougie/Airway

Exchange catheter

If Pulse is Found or Patient is Breathing, Proceed to Step 5

Step 5: ABCDE Assessment
