Step 1: Is the patient breathing?

If the patient IS breathing, apply high flow oxygen to the laryngectomy stoma. If there is any doubt whether the patient has a laryngectomy, apply oxygen to the face as well.

Assess the laryngectomy stoma patency:

  • Remove humidification cover or button if present.
  • Remove inner tube (if present).
  • Some inner tubes need re-inserting to connect to breathing circuits.
  • Do not remove a tracheoesophageal puncture (TEP) prosthesis.

Can you pass a suction catheter? If not:

  • Deflate the cuff (if present).
  • Look, listen & feel at the laryngectomy stoma or tube.
  • Use waveform capnography or Mapleson C if available.

Continue with ABCDE assessment as per the flowchart instructions.

Step 2: Assess laryngectomy stoma patency

Not all laryngectomy stomas will have a tube in situation. Remove humidification cover or button if present. Can you pass a suction catheter?

  • If no, deflate the cuff if present.
  • Look, listen, and feel at the laryngectomy stoma or tube.
  • Use waveform capnography or Mapleson C if available.
  • If the patient is not improving, consider removing the tube.

If the stoma is stable or improving, continue with ABCDE assessment and apply high flow oxygen if needed.

Step 3: Continue ABCDE assessment

Follow the ABCDE approach for patient assessment:

  • Airway: Ensure that the airway is clear and assess for any obstruction.
  • Breathing: Check the patient's breathing rate, rhythm, and depth.
  • Circulation: Assess the patient's pulse and blood pressure.
  • Disability: Check the patient's level of consciousness and any neurological deficit.
  • Exposure: Ensure the patient's body is fully exposed to check for any hidden injuries.

Step 4: Remove the tube from the laryngectomy stoma if present

Look, listen & feel at the laryngectomy stoma. Ensure oxygen is re-applied to stoma. Use Waveform capnography or Mapleson C if available.

Step 5: Is the Patient breathing?

If patient is breathing, continue with ABCDE assessment

If NOT call call Resuscitation team

Step 6: CPR if no pulse / signs of life

Primary emergency oxygenation.

Laryngectomy stoma ventilation via either Paediatric mask applied to stoma LMA applied to stoma

Step 7: Secondary emergency oxygenation

Secondary emergency oxygenation.
