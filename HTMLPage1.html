Step 1: Is the patient breathing?

If the patient IS breathing, apply high flow oxygen to the laryngectomy stoma. If there is any doubt whether the patient has a laryngectomy, apply oxygen to the face as well.

  • Apply high flow oxygen to laryngectomy stoma
  • Apply oxygent to face also if there is any doubt of patient having a laryngectomy.

If the patient is NOT breathing, take immediate action:

  • Call Resuscitation Team
  • Begin CPR if no pulse or signs of life

Step 2: Assess laryngectomy stoma patency

Not all laryngectomy stomas will have a tube in situation. Remove humidification cover or button if present. Can you pass a suction catheter?

Can you pass a suction catheter?

YES:

  • The laryngectomy stoma is patent
  • Perform tracheal suction
  • Consider partial obstruction
  • Ventilate via stoma if not breathing

NO:

  • Deflate the cuff if present.
  • Look, listen, and feel at the laryngectomy stoma or tube.
  • Use waveform capnography or Mapleson C if available.
  • If the patient is not improving, consider removing the tube.

If the stoma is stable or improving, continue with ABCDE assessment and apply high flow oxygen if needed.

If NOT, proceed to the next step and remove the tube from the laryngectomy stoma if present.

Step 3: Remove the tube from the laryngectomy stoma if present

Look, listen & feel at the laryngectomy stoma. Ensure oxygen is re-applied to stoma. Use Waveform capnography or Mapleson C if available.

Step 4: Is the Patient breathing?

Step 5: CPR if no pulse / signs of life

Primary emergency oxygenation.

Laryngectomy stoma ventilation via either Paediatric mask applied to stoma LMA applied to stoma
